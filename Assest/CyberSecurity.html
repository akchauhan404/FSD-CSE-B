Cyber Security

Cybersecurity involves protecting computer systems, networks, and data from theft, damage, or unauthorized access.

As cyber threats grow increasingly sophisticated, organizations invest in multiple layers of security measures to protect sensitive information.

Common Threats

  • Malware: Malicious software such as viruses, worms, and trojans.
  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as trustworthy entities.
  • Ransomware: Malware that locks or encrypts systems until a ransom is paid.
  • Denial-of-Service (DoS) attacks: Overloading systems to disrupt services.

Security Practices

  • Regular software updates and patching.
  • Strong password policies and multi-factor authentication.
  • Employee training to recognize social engineering attacks.
  • Robust firewall and intrusion detection systems.
